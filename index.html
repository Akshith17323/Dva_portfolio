Airline Ticket Prices vs. Fuel Costs Analysis

Problem Statement & Stakeholder Context

Jet fuel is one of the largest operating expenses for airlines (20-30%). When global crude oil prices spike due to geopolitical conflicts or macroeconomic shocks, airlines face immense margin pressure. This project investigates whether these cost increases are directly passed to consumers via ticket prices or surcharges, providing crucial insights for C-suite leaders and Revenue Management teams aiming to optimize pricing without demand loss.

Dataset Source & Scope

Integrated 6 robust datasets from sources including the EIA, IATA, and DOT. The scope encompassed monthly tracking of Brent crude and jet fuel prices, passenger fares, and fuel surcharges across distinct geopolitical phases (Pre-pandemic, COVID-19, US-Iran tensions, Russia-Ukraine conflict).

Cleaning & Transformation

Engineered an automated Python ETL pipeline to standardize columns, handle missing values, and convert data types. Implemented outlier detection (e.g., flagging 'extreme fares') and engineered specific features for downstream regression and clustering models.

KPI Framework

  • Average Ticket Price & YoY Change
  • Load Factor (Demand representation)
  • Extreme Fare % (Volatility metric)
  • Fuel Cost % of total expenses

Key Insights

  • Pricing is driven significantly more by demand and external geopolitical events than by direct fuel cost pass-through.
  • Ticket prices experience pronounced spikes specifically during conflict onset and recovery phases.
  • Fuel surcharges are leveraged strategically by airlines, not strictly on a cost-based formula.
  • Long-haul flights exhibit much higher sensitivity to fuel price fluctuations than short-haul routes.

Tableau Dashboard

An interactive Tableau dashboard was built featuring an Executive Overview, Fuel Analysis View, and Conflict Analysis View.

Recommendations & Expected Impact

  • Dynamic Pricing Strategy: Shift reliance from fuel cost to demand/seasonality forecasting. (Expected Impact: 5-10% Revenue Increase)
  • Fuel Risk Management: Adopt strategic hedging during highly volatile phases. (Expected Impact: 10-15% Cost Volatility Reduction)
  • Route-Level Optimization: Implement differentiated pricing structures for long-haul vs. short-haul. (Expected Impact: 8-12% Profitability Increase)
